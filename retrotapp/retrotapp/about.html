About Us

Richard set this up as a means to give his son Thomas an appreciation of the evolution of video games - how things compared to the games of today. He was interested to see how he reacted to simpler graphics, sound and long loading times - all of the things he happily suffered today.
The intent is not a simple "Play loads of games" thing, instead an examiniation of the games, what was good about them and what was bad about them. Then through discussion and lots of fun, they can write up a review, which will also help Thomas develop some web skills.
Of course, it's not all educational - playing these games is (generally) great fun, and satisfying Richard's need to relive his youth.

Bio: Richard

I grew up in the 80s, with a C64 being my first 'real' games machine. I loved playing platformers, beat 'em ups and football sims, and would also enjoy typing in games listings from books and magazines.
I progressed onto the Amiga, SNES, and then onto PC and Playstation, and I now play contempory games on XBOX 360 or my PC.
My all time favourite game would have to be the Football Manager series, closely followed by Super Mario World, Street Fighter II and Final Fight.
